CAD Modeling


I explored three CAD modeling softwares for making these models: AutoCAD, Fusion360, and Solidworks. In the end, I decided to use Fusion360 for its simplicity and rendering capabilities. It utilizes an easy-to-use button-based toolbar design for all actions, which was very easy to learn. However, it seemed to lack certain capabilities of more advanced softwares, such as being able to combine spline curves together, at least in an intuitive way. Modeling the corkscrew was mainly difficult because it combined cylindrical and box shapes, but Fusion360 allows smooth transitions between unlike shapes. I know that this and other CAD softwares have many more features that I have yet to begin understanding, so I aim to streamline my designing process so that I can easily model objects as simple as these in minutes rather than hours. I would specifically like to better understand the grouping features, so that I can make more accurate edits by either focusing on one section of a model or by editing multiple parts of a model simultaneously.

Corkscrew

❮
❯

This corkscrew was likely fabricated using a combination of stamping, laser cutting, and spinning techniques. Like a potter molds clay on a turntable, a spinner can mold metal into cylindrical shapes, such as a corkscrew, by spinning a metal extrusion and pushing it as it spins to force it into a cylindrical shape. The rectangular base was probably stamped into shape from a laser-cut piece of aluminum.

Bottle Opener

This bottle opener was most likely fabricated stamping and laser cutting aluminum metal. By using large amounts of force, stamping machines can bend stiff metals into specific shapes and forms. To achieve the unique design and profile of the bottle opener, it was likely laser cut according to a vector file.
